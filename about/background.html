Background & Goals

University of Miami’s Laboratory for INtegrative Knowledge (ULINK) supports teams of scholars from multiple disciplines in collaborative, problem-based inquiry to address the complex challenges of society.

The ULINK Coastal Resilience team merges expertise from ocean sciences, structural coastal engineering, marine ecology & biology, architecture & urban planning, and communication, building a truly interdisciplinary approach to find coastal resilience solutions that lie at the intersection of shoreline, humans, and habitat.


Our goal is to create a science-based design framework to develop and test coral restoration strategies that combine gray (cement-based) and green (nature-based) defenses to reduce the vulnerability of coastal communities to the impacts of ocean waves and wave-driven flooding.

Acknowledgements

Our research is possible due to the sustained support we have received from our partners. The City of Miami Beach Sustainability and Resiliency Committee and Communications Team have been our champions with various aspects of the project, especially in launching our permit applications and facilitating our engagement and communications research with Miami Beach residents. Experts from The Nature Conservancy have also provided consultation to us to assist us in modeling the benefits of restoration for coastal protection in Miami Beach. University of Miami’s Laboratory for INtegrative Knowledge (ULINK) has provided funding support for this project, allowing us to make synergistic use of various areas of research and practice (reef restoration, wave dynamics, structural design, and urban vulnerability), into a multifacted single project seeking solutions for coastal resilience.
