Coastal Vulnerabilities

As a long-time magnet for coastal development, South Florida has experienced explosive population growth in the last few decades. The desire of many residents to live along the coast exposes them to the direct impacts of ocean waves, storm surge, tidal inundation, beach erosion, and sea-level rise. Recent impacts of hurricanes Harvey, Irma, and Maria, which have caused loss of lives and hundreds of billions of dollars in losses in the US and the Caribbean, have highlighted the crucial need to explore effective and cost-efficient methods to decrease such threats.

Given these conditions, protecting residents and the local economy has taken center stage in the social, cultural, and political life of Florida. The University of Miami is uniquely qualified and strategically located to take a leading role in developing science-based strategies to enhance the resilience of our high-value but vulnerable coastline.
